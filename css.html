Cinnamon Babka Rolls

ingredients: milk, bread flour, salt, brown sugar, granulated sugar yeast, butter (unsalted), cinnamon powder

instructions: Make a tangzhong, add 170 grams of milk and 35 grams of bread flour into a small pot and whisk until no lumps remain. Cover over medium heat, stirring regularly, until thickened and spatula leaves a line in the bottom of the pot. Remove from heat and place tangzhong into a stand-mixer mixing bowl.

For the dough, add 277g of cold milk, 450g bread flour, 9g salt, 38g sugar, 9g yeast, 86g soft butter to the tangzhong and mix on low speed with a dough hook until gluten window pane develops. Form the dough into a ball, place in a oiled bowl and cover to rest in fridge for 3 to 4 hours.

Roll out the dough into a large rectanglar shape. Once the dough is rolled out, spread 120g of softened butter onto the dough.

For the filling, mix together 300g brown sugar and 12g cinnamon. Once mixed, spread the mixture onto the buttered dough. Roll the dough up and use a bread knife to cut approximately 3-inch sections (9 rolls). Seal each section and place into a well oiled muffin pan. Place muffin pan on a tray and wrap securely with plastic wrap and chill in fridge overnight.

The next day, to bake preheat your oven to 375F, sprinkle each roll with granulated sugar before baking. Then bake for 14 minutes, rotating the tray at 7 minutes.

Castella (Sponge Cake)

ingredients: eggs, granulated sugar, honey, bread flour, vanilla extract

instructions: Whip 240g of egg whites using a stand mixer on level 1-2 speed (never 3). When frothy, add 220g granulated sugar little by little. Continue whipping until firm, but not stiff peaks.

On speed 1, add 160g egg yolks and 126g honey slowly.

Additionally, on speed 1, sift 200g bread flour in parts into the mixture until just incorporated.

Portion batter into oiled and lined pans. Spread. Then bang firmly on countertop to release large air bubles.

Preheat and bake at 325F for 12-14 minutes.

Remove from oven. Top pan with a wooden board and firmly bang on countertop to allow top to level. Remove the board and let cool before removing the cake from the pan.
